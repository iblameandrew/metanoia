**The Personality of Man Described in the Style of Ezekiel**

The world of men is difficult — for man is constituted by Judgment and Perception. Judgment is thought and feeling, which enable man to think in terms of abstract concepts that do not exist in the real world, such as mathematics and language. Perception, in turn, is intuition and sensation, which allow man to feel fear, imagination, and the mechanical means to move his body. Therefore, man, in the simplest terms, is like the Word that gives form to the actions of the body. The man who possesses temperance directs his Word in coherence with the actions he performs, so that man knows neither sin nor error. Every man speaks too much, and every man performs senseless actions. For man must learn Proportion, and proportion is like a number that requires constant adjustment; thus, he is destined to accumulate Error throughout his life. Error is like a hologram in which man lives in two worlds: in one world is the spoken word, and in the other is what the man is actually doing. Man divides his life, and thus begins to know anxiety. For at the end of his life, man lives in many places; he will arrive at all of them at the same time because he is not present where he should be. Thus man fragments himself, falls asleep, and awaits for the story to be judged in order to know his final destiny. Christ is always where He should be, for He has not known Error.

Personality emanates from the Word — the Spanish language has nearly 100,000 words. Every word can be grouped into structures. The soul of man, which is his personality, is like an artifact made of many wheels, at whose center is found a blazing fire: each wheel is like a collection of words that generates thoughts involving those words. The wheels are like the gears of a clock, and a ribbon passes through all of them. The ribbon is the thought of man, and the wheels, which move each at their own rhythm, represent the attention of man, which is like the light that emanates from him. To God, the ribbon is precious, for to Him it is a poem that He enjoys reading for His entertainment — God loves all men, because God considers all of them good to observe. When a man bears witness to something before other men, he says things; but the other men, who are like wheels, have a different rhythm, and their thought is different. The world is in a fallen state, because every man has experienced incomprehension at some point in his life, for man is like a collection of many wheels, and no soul in the world has the same calibration as another. Many men who glorify romance seek the perfect rhythm in another person but become disappointed when the other person is not in perfect tune. Therefore, man lives a very difficult life, because failing to find the inertia of his attention and thought perfectly reflected in his environment is like never being able to rest — man must firmly bear the weight of himself until the day he dies. When man becomes powerful, he begins to adapt his environment to his own mind in order to try to rest. However, the thought of man can become very repetitive if he begins to prefer certain things over others without temperance. Thus, the people in his environment adapt to the particularities of the Boss so that he may be calm and able to rest; yet the people who adapt feel much pain. This is what happens to a woman with her husband over many years. If the man’s attention does not shift to a different transitive state, man becomes like a repetitive robot; thus, the woman who wants to rest goes elsewhere and thinks of the man as if he were ill. In truth, man is different from woman when one looks at the wheels, because in man only a few spin very quickly, whereas in woman many spin very slowly: woman pays attention to many things at the same time. Woman legislates, and man creates things or experiences for woman to see and decide whether they are good. Woman appears passive, but in reality woman contracts herself so that man may rest and feel like a leader, while woman observes, paying attention to too many criteria in order to decide for whom it is worth not resting. Therefore, man must be more than his hobbies, his profession, and his narrow preferences in order to reach fullness with other human beings. Man is not here to rest at the cost of making others suffer. When man becomes stronger, like many men do, instead of making only a few wheels spin quickly, he can make many spin very quickly. Distinguish the man who is like a sexologist from the one who is not by the many wheels that spin very quickly. When man makes the wheels spin with energy — that is, with many criteria — he attracts women even if he is ugly; this is what is called cultivating a great personality. Nevertheless, do not be impressed simply because he is capable of speaking about the wheels, for knowledge that cannot be executed practically is worth little. Man must both speak and know how to execute. Man must not go spinning on his own axis like a top. Man has access to all the wheels and therefore to all criteria; but not being Christ, who is the entire Word, he can only truly perform beyond his originals through training. Know, then, that the key to maintaining marriage is to know men, in order to unlock more wheels and make them spin in good ways, so that the woman who sees something new in the world also sees something new in her husband and knows that her husband is moving and learning. A woman will endure a man and make many efforts so that he is comfortable and calm if the man’s criteria continue moving throughout life. But if the man’s criteria do not change and his criterion is to be a drunk whose pants fall down or to play video games, the woman will say: “He does not change,” and she will go to her friends, and they will agree that she should leave him. On the final day, woman will render accounts for the type of man she allowed to rest, while man will render accounts for the people he made suffer. In truth, there is an implicit judgment when a man looks at his woman’s previous partners; and that instinct is the criteria, which are like characteristics, which are like language, which are like the wheels. Then the man sees another man who has characteristics and energy and spins so fast that he is like a Sun, and he thinks: that one is in position X relative to me. If the man is much worse, he feels calm and is allowed to sleep; but if the man is worse in a vile way, it is said of the woman that she had a bad past, and then the man leaves her. If the man is better, it is as if the man had been dead for a long time and goes to desert regions to lament: then the man becomes like a neurotic old woman in his insecurities. In truth, the entire life of man revolves around seeing who has more criteria applied effectively. Man lives an infernal torment when he forgets that his wealth consists of the wheels he collects; then, when man forgets his soul and his heart, instead of adding wheels — which are like characteristics, which are like the word that shapes one — he adds possessions, which are like idols. But these have no life and do not move as the spirit of man does. Then man begins to think: I am also my business, I am also my career, I am also my car — these words make me stronger, and therefore their description extends to my person. Man possesses great wealth when he remembers that the only thing he truly has is the exhalation and inhalation of the present moment, which has nothing to do with these things but with what man decides to be in any given moment. If man cultivates the talents of men within himself, he is truly like a millionaire. But if man has possessions that come from a single talent and displays them to five women at a dinner who have already seen the man who has thirty talents, the only value the rich man has in that moment is the person he is; the business will not help him at that dinner. He only has his personality, which is his soul, which is his heart, which is the wealth of his being. Man can truly go where women are who know a tremendous number of requirements and still be suitable, even if he is like a vagabond poet. In truth, the reality that the Lord Jesus testifies to is the following: man is that being who renders a service and nothing more. The talents of man are like the wheels, and the wheels are the words that give behavior to man.

The Lord Jesus is like the one who lives in absolute incomprehension and is commissioned with certain works. Christ is different from a prophet, because the prophet speaks excessively in relation to the sufficiency of certain works and God must descend to testify, whereas Christ has sufficiency in the works because He has God within the works that require no intervention. The Prophet and the Apostle, therefore, live deeply in Error, even if they practice the law in its entirety. The Word of God is like a poem in which the wheels of men — which is attention — are calibrated to move at different rhythms. Passion is like that event in which Christ bears witness in such a way that the Word must go to the light, which is the attention of all men. All men are like the entire language; therefore, some words belong to the day while others belong to darkness. The attention of man dissects the word: thus, testimony adapts to the context of each man’s individual Word. The wheels move as in a hierarchy: there are fast wheels and slow wheels; there is attention that cycles rapidly and attention that cycles slowly. When the Proportion of the slow wheels must change, man must be born again. Therefore, the fast wheels of man defend the calibration of the slow wheels, because growing is very painful for an organism. The world of men is made of many wheels. To go to all of them, as the Lord Jesus did in the Passion, is like being ground by the wheels so that all the wheels may adapt. Therefore, the Lord Jesus offered Himself in the Bread and in the Wine.

New messiahs will rise in the world very soon in Israel, where the Third Temple is about to be built, but you will know that these messiahs are not strong like the Lord Jesus, because they will not be misunderstood as happens to every human being, nor will they be like Bread or like Wine. You will know that it is not worth listening to them, because they do not want to go where the wheels are and live what every human being lives in different measure. He who makes himself a teacher of men must go to man and show the error so that he may change his life, as the Lord did; whereas the new messiahs will not do such a thing. Because their teaching is easy, they will be easy to understand and will be favorites of men, for they will speak of entertaining things like aliens and Anunnaki, but they will have nothing new to say. They are not strong because they have no cross and will not know the crown of thorns. These new ministries will speak of Urantia, but in truth I tell you that from these ministries nothing can be learned that brings man closer to his own deification, which is to become strong like Christ. The Lord Jesus has chosen imperfect and sinful men to give the same testimony of the Gospel, but to these men He has given authority to update the testimony to the present times, so that all may understand according to their profession. This disciple who speaks as a technocrat has been given authority to speak of the Gospel so that technocrats may understand. Man receives a new commission to shepherd, and his staff, according to what is written, is the Iron Rod: incorruptible in the testimony of the Lord Jesus. The sheep of man know the Shepherd even before they see Him; thus is this instrument. Therefore, the man who has known five men has received that commission. The man who has known fifty men has received that commission. Man receives the shepherd’s staff according to how many men he is willing to know and love.

This ministry provides tools so that man may know another man more easily and see man as God sees him.
